Finding a Toilet on the Road

— a nomad's guide to bathroom hunting —
• • • • •
⛽
Truck Stops free
Love's, Pilot Flying J, and TA are open 24/7, clean, and used to travelers. Best bet for late-night or early-morning runs.
🛒
Big Box & Grocery Stores free
Walmart, Target, and grocery chains almost always have public restrooms near the entrance or back of the store.
📚
Public Libraries free
Clean, quiet, and often have wifi and AC too — a great combo stop for a bathroom break and some downtime.
🌲
Rest Areas & Welcome Centers free
Along most highways, maintained and marked with signs well in advance — plan stops around them on long drives.
🏨
Hotel & Casino Lobbies free
Walk in like you belong there. Casinos in particular tend to have nice, always-open restrooms.
☕
Coffee Shops & Fast Food buy something small
Starbucks, McDonald's, and similar chains usually have restrooms — grabbing a cheap coffee keeps it courteous.
🏕️
State Parks & National Forests $0–$10 entry
Look for vault toilets at trailheads and campgrounds — no plumbing, but reliably stocked and maintained.
📱
Bathroom-Finder Apps free
Apps like Flush and iExit crowdsource public restroom locations, ratings, and hours near you.
Pro tip: Keep a small "just in case" kit in the van — hand sanitizer, wipes, a roll of TP, and a portable toilet or trowel for true middle-of-nowhere moments.
