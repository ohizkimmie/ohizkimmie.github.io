Best Domicile States for Nomads

— no state income tax + nomad-friendly rules = your legal "home base" —
• • • • •
The Big Three
🌴
Florida No min. stay
No income, estate, or inheritance tax. No minimum physical presence requirement — file a Declaration of Domicile with your county clerk, get a FL license, and you're set. Strong asset protection laws and the most popular pick with full-timers in 2026, partly for its major-airport access.
🤠
Texas 30-day stay
No income tax, plus constitutional (not just legislative) protection against one ever being added. Home base of the Escapees RV Club in Livingston, TX, which is set up to help full-timers register vehicles and handle jury duty by mail. Downsides: a 30-day initial presence to establish, and annual vehicle safety inspections.
🦬
South Dakota 1-night stay
Long the go-to for full-timers — just one overnight stay, a mailbox address, and a same-day DMV visit gets you a license. Cheapest vehicle registration of the three. Heads up: 2025's HB 1208 now restricts nomads using mail-forwarding addresses to federal-election-only voting, so you'd lose your state/local ballot.
Other No-Income-Tax States
🎰
Nevada
No income tax and a Vegas hub with easy international flight access — good for entrepreneurs, but far less built-out mail-forwarding and DMV infrastructure for nomads than the Big Three.
🐎
Wyoming
No income tax and the best privacy/asset-protection laws around for LLCs and trusts. Popular with entrepreneurs, but minimal nomad-specific mail or DMV support compared to FL/TX/SD.
🎸
Tennessee
Fully repealed its old tax on interest and dividends, so it's genuinely zero income tax now. Just doesn't have the established nomad mail-forwarding ecosystem that Texas, Florida, and South Dakota do.
🌲
Washington Caution
No tax on wages, but a capital gains excise tax now applies above a set threshold — worth a second look if you have investments or plan to sell assets.
How to actually make it official: Get a residential address in your new state (mail-forwarding service works), get a driver's license there and surrender your old one, register your vehicle, and register to vote. Then sever ties with your old state — courts look at your whole life picture (address, banking, licenses), not just one form.
Not legal or tax advice — domicile rules are fact-specific and state laws keep changing. Talk to a CPA or tax attorney before making the switch.
